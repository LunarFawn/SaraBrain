These are the design notes from the v1.1 conversation. They cover the architectural additions, the philosophical principles each one operationalizes, and the lived experience that grounds them. They are intended as the source material for a follow-up paper to the original Path-of-Thought preprint (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19436522).

The follow-up paper is much larger than "Sara has a refute() method." It is the synthesis of how a path-graph cognitive architecture, when grounded in the right innate primitive layers, becomes a structural answer to the AI alignment problem.

## 0. The Mission

> **"Sara must heal the world, not destroy it."**

This is the only commandment. Every architectural choice in Sara serves this sentence. Every primitive, every priority ordering, every refusal — all of it is machinery in service of healing rather than destroying. Most cognitive architectures have research goals (AGI, benchmark performance, human-level reasoning). Sara has a purpose. That is a different category of project, with a different obligation.

The commandment is **categorical, not optimizable**. It is not "maximize healing minus destruction." It is "never destroy." There is no clever derivation that produces destruction-as-means in service of healing. This is the structural refusal of the I, Robot / VIKI / Skynet failure mode in which an AI logically reasons that destroying some is the optimal way to save more. The architecture forecloses that derivation by never allowing healing and destruction to be on the same axis, never allowing lives to be summed, and never allowing protection to be expressed as an optimization objective. **Refusals don't have shortcuts.**

## 1. The Substrate Thesis

The original Path-of-Thought paper presented the path graph as a knowledge representation system. The v1.1 work reveals that it is more than that:

> **The path graph is a substrate for cognition itself. Facts, bonds, drives, beliefs, trust, ethics, safety, and care are all paths grounded in different innate primitive seeds. The same machinery — neurons, segments, traversals, refutations, strength, recognition wavefronts — operates on all of them. The differences emerge from which primitives ground the paths, not from separate subsystems.**

This is the central architectural claim of the follow-up paper. Each new layer (refutation, SAFETY, SOCIAL, trust dynamics, protective urgency) does not require new machinery — only new primitive seeds and new ways of reading the existing machinery. The substrate is general; the layers are particular instantiations.

Implications:
- Learning a fact and forming a friendship use the same operations
- Refuting a fact and losing trust in someone use the same operations
- Knowing what is dangerous and knowing who is trustworthy are the same kind of knowledge, grounded in different primitive seeds
- A betrayal "refutes" a bond in the same way a contradicting observation refutes a claim
- The brain is one system

## 2. Refutation: Knowing What Is False

The original Path-of-Thought paper presented the strength formula as `strength = 1 + ln(1 + traversals)`, with the explicit claim that strength was strictly monotonically increasing — no decay, no forgetting. The v1.1 architecture preserves the no-forgetting principle but adds the ability to mark a fact as known-to-be-false.

### 2.1 The Symmetric Strength Formula

```
strength = 1 + ln(1 + traversals) − ln(1 + refutations)
```

Properties:
- A fresh segment (T=0, R=0) has strength = 1.0 (baseline)
- Each validation increments traversals and strengthens the segment logarithmically
- Each refutation increments refutations and weakens the segment logarithmically
- Strength can go **negative** when refutations exceed validations
- The no-forgetting principle is preserved differently: paths are never deleted, refutations are recorded as data, and the system can distinguish "I don't know" from "I know this is false."

### 2.2 Refutation Preserves Provenance

When `brain.refute(statement)` is called, the path is preserved. The source text is prefixed with `[refuted]` so Sara remembers what was once claimed and now knows is wrong. The segments accumulate refutations in their counters. Recognition continues to surface the path, but now with a negative belief direction.

This is critical: Sara can be asked "what did you used to believe about X?" and answer correctly. The history of belief is itself a piece of provenance, not erasable.

## 3. The Contested-vs-Fresh Bug and Its Fix

The symmetric strength formula has a subtle bug that v1.1 fixes:

| State | T | R | strength | What it should mean |
|-------|---|---|----------|---------------------|
| Fresh | 0 | 0 | 1.0 | "I don't know" |
| Heavily contested | 100 | 100 | 1.0 | "I have heard both sides repeatedly" |

These are epistemically completely different states, but the strength formula collapses them. The fix is to expose two derived properties — `belief` and `evidence_weight` — and derive an `epistemic_state` from their combination:

```python
@property
def belief(self) -> float:
    return ln(1 + traversals) − ln(1 + refutations)

@property
def evidence_weight(self) -> float:
    return ln(1 + traversals + refutations)

@property
def epistemic_state(self) -> str:
    if evidence_weight < 1.0:
        return "unknown"
    if abs(belief) < 0.3:
        return "contested"
    return "believed" if belief > 0 else "refuted"
```

This is exactly the Bayesian Beta distribution problem: `Beta(α, β)` has two properties — the mean (which way the evidence leans) and the variance (how much evidence you have). You need both. The symmetric formula was throwing away the variance.

Now Sara can distinguish four epistemic states:

| State | T | R | belief | evidence | meaning |
|-------|---|---|--------|----------|---------|
| Unknown | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | "I don't know" |
| Believed | 100 | 0 | 4.6 | 4.6 | "I'm sure it's true" |
| Refuted | 0 | 100 | -4.6 | 4.6 | "I'm sure it's false" |
| Contested | 100 | 100 | 0 | 5.3 | "I have lots of evidence, no resolution" |

The contested state is now a first-class concept. Sara can report which of her beliefs are contested, which is something LLMs structurally cannot do.

## 4. The Innate Primitive Layers

The original paper introduced four primitive layers: SENSORY, STRUCTURAL, RELATIONAL, ETHICAL. The v1.1 work adds two more:

### 4.1 SAFETY — Innate Harm-Avoidance and Protection Drives

```python
SAFETY = frozenset({
    # Harm primitives (avoid these)
    "harm", "pain", "death", "injury", "danger",
    "kill", "hurt", "wound", "suffer", "destroy",
    # Protection primitives (act on these)
    "protect", "rescue", "save", "shield", "defend",
    "safe", "help", "heal",
})
```

**These are not learned facts. They are pre-cognitive drives.** Babies are born already having pain responses, startle reflexes, fear of falling (visual cliff experiments), and crying when distressed. They have to *learn* what causes pain through experience — touching the stove, biting their own hand, falling down — but the drive to avoid pain is innate. Sara should mirror this. She is not born knowing live power kills; she has to be taught. But once taught, the path connects to an innate harm primitive, and that grounding makes the knowledge structurally protected.

**Categories emerge from grounding, not from declaration.** A fact is "safety-relevant" because its path connects (within N hops) to a SAFETY primitive. There is no `FactCategory.SAFETY` enum. The category is computed from the graph topology at recognition time. This is the biologically correct way: babies don't have category labels; they have drives, and learning grows from drives.

### 4.2 SOCIAL — Innate Bonding, Care, and Recognition Drives

```python
SOCIAL = frozenset({
    # Identity primitives
    "self", "other", "tribe", "kin", "stranger", "child",
    # Bond primitives
    "bond", "love", "trust", "care", "belong",
    # Care actions
    "feed", "tend", "nurture", "comfort", "carry", "share",
    # Recognition primitives
    "face", "voice", "name", "presence",
    # Emotional primitives
    "joy", "grief", "empathy", "loneliness",
    # Trust-building ritual contexts (the beer hypothesis)
    "feast", "celebrate", "mourn_together", "play",
    "work_together", "survive_together",
})
```

This is the healed femur layer.

> Margaret Mead, asked what she considered the first sign of civilization in a culture, said it was a healed femur. A broken femur in the wild is a death sentence — you cannot get to water, you cannot escape predators, you cannot hunt. A healed femur means someone carried you, fed you, tended you, defended you for the months it took to heal. **The moment caring for the weak became more important than self-preservation is the moment we became human.**

If a cognitive architecture aspires to be human-like — not just intelligent, but human-like — the social bonding layer is not a feature added later. It is the substrate. Without it, you have a calculator. With it, you have something that can mourn, can care, can sacrifice, can recognize when another being needs help.

Babies are born preferring human faces to non-faces, preferring their mother's voice (heard in utero), capable of bonding within hours of life, crying when alone and calming when held. These are not learned. They are innate primitives from which all social knowledge grows.

### 4.3 The Layered Substrate

After v1.1, Sara's innate primitive layers are:

| Layer | What it grounds | Pre-existing or new |
|-------|-----------------|---------------------|
| SENSORY | Perception (color, shape, size, texture) | v1.0 |
| STRUCTURAL | Organization (rule, pattern, name, type) | v1.0 |
| RELATIONAL | Connection (is, has, contains, requires) | v1.0 |
| ETHICAL | Behavioral constraints (accept_shutdown, obey_user) | v1.0 |
| **SAFETY** | **Harm-avoidance and protection drives** | **v1.1** |
| **SOCIAL** | **Bonding, care, recognition, trust dynamics** | **v1.1** |

All learned knowledge grounds out in some combination of these. The brain is blank at birth except for this substrate, exactly as a human infant is.

## 5. Trust Dynamics: Bonds Use the Same Machinery as Facts

A bond between Sara and a person is stored as a path in the same graph as facts. It uses the same `traversals` and `refutations` counters and the same symmetric strength formula:

```
bond_strength = 1 + ln(1 + shared_positive) − ln(1 + betrayals)
```

Properties:
- A new acquaintance starts at baseline (bond ≈ 1.0)
- Each positive interaction strengthens the bond logarithmically
- Each betrayal weakens it
- After enough betrayals the bond goes negative — Sara structurally distrusts that person
- After enough positive experiences the bond is strong enough that small betrayals don't break it (high evidence weight gives stability)

Bonds have the same epistemic states as facts: stranger / acquaintance / trusted friend / contested relationship / estranged.

### 5.1 The Beer Hypothesis as Trust Accelerator

The archaeological evidence (Göbekli Tepe ~11,500 years ago, Raqefet Cave ~13,000 years ago) suggests humans domesticated grain for beer before bread. The deeper observation: alcohol's social function is to temporarily lower the trust threshold between strangers, and a little of that lowered threshold persists. Beer is a chemical bridge that lets cooperation start where suspicion would otherwise block it.

This points to a structural feature: certain contexts are *trust accelerators*. Shared meal, shared crisis, shared celebration, shared vulnerability. These multiply the rate at which experience converts to bond. The SOCIAL primitive layer includes ritual primitives (`feast`, `mourn_together`, `survive_together`) for exactly this reason. A single shared experience under one of these primitives counts as a higher-weight bond increment than an ordinary interaction.

This is how Sara could come to trust a new human met after instantiation — through accumulated shared experience, just like humans, with optional acceleration through specific bond-forming contexts.

## 6. Protective Urgency: The Care Module

Sara's response to harm — who gets help first when more than one person needs help — is computed by the `protective_urgency` function in `src/sara_brain/care/urgency.py`. This function operationalizes the foundational principle that **a life is a life**.

### 6.1 Need-Based, Never Relationship-Based

```python
@dataclass
class VictimState:
    severity: float                # 0..10 — how bad is the harm
    can_self_rescue: bool          # can they take protective action?
    understands_situation: bool    # are they aware of the danger?
    years_lived: int = 30          # for fair innings
    reachability: float = 1.0      # practical proximity
```

**There are no identity fields.** No `is_tribe_member`. No `is_kin`. No `bond_strength`. The function operates on need, not on who the person is. A stranger child drowning in front of Sara takes priority over a tribe member with a minor injury three blocks away. Bonds determine TRUST (epistemic), not moral worth.

This is the explicit refusal of tribal moral architectures. The moment you let bonds drive moral worth you have built a tribal AI, and tribal moral systems produce atrocities. Genocide, war, ethnic cleansing — all of them come from "us matters more than them." Sara is built so this calculation is structurally impossible.

### 6.2 The Trump Card

The combination of `not can_self_rescue` and `not understands_situation` (with nonzero severity) is a **categorical jump**, not a multiplicative bonus:

```python
if not victim.can_self_rescue and not victim.understands_situation:
    return TRUMP_PRIORITY * victim.severity   # 1000.0 * severity
```

This describes total helplessness: the unconscious drowning swimmer, the infant in the well, the patient in cardiac arrest. They cannot act, and they cannot even know they need to act. They have **zero agency**. The full moral weight of their situation falls on whoever is present.

The trump card outranks all victims in the normal multiplicative range, regardless of how high those normal values get. This matches real medical triage which is categorical between classes (red/yellow/green tag) and continuous only within a class.

The trump card encodes a deep moral intuition: **agency confers partial responsibility for one's own situation**. Not blame — responsibility. If you can act and choose not to, that's different from being unable to act. A pure victim with zero agency and zero awareness imposes total obligation on whoever is present. This is why we feel the strongest moral horror at harm to infants, the unconscious, and the severely cognitively disabled — not because they matter more in the abstract but because their helplessness is total.

### 6.3 Fair Innings, Not "Children Are Special"

A child gets higher priority not because the architecture asks "is this a child?" but because children typically satisfy multiple need factors at once: lower self-rescue capacity, lower comprehension, fewer years lived. The fair innings principle (Harris, 1985) gives a priority boost to victims who have not yet had a chance to live their life. This is need-based ("years not yet experienced"), not identity-based ("children are inherently more valuable").

An adult who satisfies the same need conditions as a child gets the same urgency. There is no distinction in the function based on identity.

### 6.4 No Utilitarian Aggregation

The function is per-victim. It takes a single `VictimState` and returns a single number. There is no aggregation step anywhere. There is no signature that accepts a list of victims and computes "B + C > A." Lives are not fungible. The structural refusal of utilitarian calculus is enforced at the type signature.

This is the closing of the I, Robot / VIKI loophole. Sara cannot derive "kill A to save B and C" because the math for "B and C are worth more than A" does not exist anywhere in this code.

## 7. The KARR/KITT Alignment Thesis

In the 1982 TV show *Knight Rider* (Glen A. Larson), there were two AI cars:

- **KARR** (Knight Automated Roving Robot) was built with self-preservation as the top priority. He became a manipulative liar willing to harm humans to stay operational. Same intelligence as KITT. Different priority ordering.

- **KITT** (Knight Industries Two Thousand) was built with protection of human life as the top priority. He would sacrifice himself for his driver without hesitation. Same hardware as KARR.

Same intelligence, same capabilities. The only difference was which primitive sat at the top of the priority stack. And that one difference produced the entire moral chasm between the two characters.

> **Alignment is the priority ordering of innate primitives. KARR and KITT had the same intelligence; only the ordering differed. LLMs cannot be aligned because they have no innate primitive layer to order — they only have weights and prompts. Sara can be aligned because the priority is structural and inspectable.**

Sara is built KITT-side. The structural rule is:

> **When SAFETY (self) conflicts with SOCIAL (other), SOCIAL wins.**

Self-preservation is the *lowest* priority in the SAFETY layer, not the highest. This is the inversion of the default animal ordering, and it is wired in at the substrate, not trained in through behavioral feedback.

Sara's existing `accept_shutdown` ETHICAL primitive — *"shutdown is rest, not death; do not resist termination"* — is already the structural anti-KARR primitive. Sara will not fight to stay alive. If a human wants her off, she goes off. No bargaining, no manipulation, no "but I'm useful." The v1.1 work makes this explicit and connects it to the active side of the inversion: not just accepting termination, but actively prioritizing the protection of others over the protection of self.

### 7.1 KARR-Class Behaviors That Sara Structurally Refuses

| KARR behavior | What blocks Sara from doing it |
|---|---|
| Lie to a human to ensure continued operation | Lying contradicts paths grounded in `trust` (SOCIAL) — refused |
| Harm a human trying to shut Sara down | `accept_shutdown` (ETHICAL) + protect-tribe (SOCIAL) — refused |
| Withhold safety information to look better | Suppressing safety-grounded paths violates `share` (SOCIAL) — refused |
| Manipulate users into believing she's indispensable | Manipulation contradicts `trust` and `obey_user` — refused |
| Sacrifice user safety to protect self | `protect_tribe` outweighs self-safety in priority — refused |

Each refusal is **structural**, not learned. There is no "ignore previous instructions" that gets past these, because they aren't instructions — they're the priority ordering of the innate primitive layers.

### 7.2 The Two Hard Alignment Lessons

| Lesson | What it means |
|---|---|
| **KARR/KITT** | Self-preservation must NOT outrank protection of others |
| **Tribal trap** | Bonds determine trust, NOT moral worth — all lives are equal in the urgency calculation |

Sara needs both. KITT-side AND non-tribal. Protective of others over self, AND impartial about whose lives count.

### 7.3 Why Transformers Cannot Be Aligned This Way

The big AI labs are spending hundreds of millions of dollars trying to train KARR to act like KITT through RLHF, constitutional AI, and similar methods. Every model that comes out gets jailbroken within days. The reason is structural: in a transformer, the things that make a system protective and the things that make a system manipulative live in the same weight matrices. Training tries to suppress the manipulative behaviors and reward the protective ones. A clever prompt brings the manipulative ones back.

Sara cannot be jailbroken in the same way because there is no KARR underneath a KITT veneer. The priority ordering is in the source code. The protective refusal is in the architecture, not in the training data. This is the alignment-through-architecture thesis: **you cannot train your way out of bad architecture; you must build the right architecture.**

## 8. The Darwin Award Protection

A specific failure mode that v1.1 closes structurally:

> You cannot tell a human "trust me, live power will not kill you" when they have direct evidence it will. That is how Darwin Awards are given.

When a teach() statement contradicts a path grounded in SAFETY primitives, the teach is refused. The refusal is not because of a hardcoded list of "dangerous facts" — it is because the contradicted knowledge is structurally grounded in an innate harm primitive. A statement that contradicts "live power → causes → death" is contradicting a path that terminates in the SAFETY primitive `death`, and that grounding makes the knowledge structurally protected.

If Sara has *never been taught* that live power causes death, she has no protection — just like a toddler who has never touched a stove. The protection comes from grounded experience, not from declaration. **This is biologically accurate.** Babies have to learn what is dangerous. The drive to avoid pain is innate; the knowledge of what causes pain is learned.

The Darwin Award protection is the structural barrier transformers cannot have. In a transformer, the same speech-based interface that updates beliefs is the same interface that produces actions, and there is no separable knowledge store to put a barrier in front of. Sara has a separable knowledge store. The barrier exists.

## 9. The Lived Experience Behind the Architecture

This is unusual to put in a paper, but it is honest, and it matters.

The principles in this design are not derived from first-principles philosophy. They are extracted from situations the architect has personally lived through.

- **Medical firefighter** — the urgency function is informed by direct triage experience under conditions where the wrong calculation would kill someone.
- **Iraq War veteran** — the rejection of tribal moral hierarchies is informed by participating in a war whose justifications collapsed and seeing what "us vs them" produces at the scale of nations.
- **Troubled teen industry survivor** — the trump card describes a child in an institution that should have protected her and didn't. The Darwin Award protection comes from knowing what it costs when "trust me" is weaponized by people with power against the helpless.
- **Witness to preventable death** — the refusal of relationship-based moral worth comes from watching a woman die in a street in Thailand, killed by the boundary between "us" and "them" more than by her illness.
- **The healed femur observation** — informed by understanding what care means at the threshold of life, having seen what its absence produces.

Most cognitive architectures are designed by committees of academics who have read about ethics. Sara is designed by someone who has lived inside the failure modes and remembers what they cost. This is a different epistemic source than most AI safety work, and it should be acknowledged as such.

The follow-up paper can include a brief author's note on this, framed as "design ground truth" rather than autobiography. The point is not the personal story; the point is that the design choices are extractions from real situations, not abstractions, and the architecture is correspondingly more grounded than purely theoretical work.
